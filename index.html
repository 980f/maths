  • Savitsky-Golay made fast
    Savitsky-Golay refers to two scientists who published a paper on fitting data to polynomials as a digital filter
    My interest was in finding peaks in XRF spectra, for calibrating the energy to channel gain of our detection system.
    My first implementation target was an 80.effing.51 microcontroller and I had to write it as a language extension to a BASIC interpreter. I was able to determine that the maximum range of the values involved was with 24 bits, which did mean that I had to write 24 bit math routines for this 8 bit suitable-for-controlling-washing-machines processor.
    Not in these papers (yet) is the interpolating of a fractional coordinate value for the zero crossing, which was critical to machine precision when the peak find was done on every sample, not just on well managed calibration standards.
